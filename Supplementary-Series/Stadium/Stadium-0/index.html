Pokémon Stadium 0

Pokémon Stadium 0 was released in 1998 for the Nintendo 64, in Japan exclusively. Although it was the first Stadium game to be released, it was never released in English-speaking territories. Because this game predates the English-language Pokémon Stadium, it is often referred to by English-speaking fans as either Pokémon Stadium 0 or "Pocket Monsters Stadium". I will be using the Stadium 0 designation.

In this game, the player can use Pokémon from their (Japanese) copies of Red, Green, Blue, or Yellow to battle other human players or computer-controlled opponents. There are also rental Pokémon that the player can use, but their stats and movesets are usually inferior to those that could be created from a copy of one of the Game Boy games. It is, however, possible to complete the game's two tournament cups using only rental Pokémon, although it does rely on luck.

In addition to battling, the player can also interact with a connected copy of a Game Boy Pokémon game in a variety of ways. The player can:

  • Organize their boxes and party on their Game Boy game, and transfer Pokémon back and forth between their Game Boy game and Stadium 0
  • View their Pokémon's stats
  • View their Pokédex with 3D models
  • Register a team of Pokémon for use in the battles
  • Play the connected Game Boy game on the TV
