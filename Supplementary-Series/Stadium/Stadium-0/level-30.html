Stadium 0 Level 1-30 Division

The Level 1-30 Division challenge is a series of four cups, named after the four types of Poké Balls available in Red, Green, Blue, and Yellow. Within each cup, the player will have to defeat eight Trainers in a row without ever losing to one of the Trainers. The Trainers in each cup are identical and use the same Pokémon species, but the Pokémon's stats, levels, and moves improve with each cup.

The player can choose to use either a team selected from rental Pokémon, or a team consisting of the player's current party in a connected Game Boy game. The only way that players can use both rental Pokémon and Game Boy Pokémon on the same team is if their Game Boy team contains two Pokémon of the same species. As duplicate species are forbidden, the player will be prompted to replace one of the duplicates with a rental Pokémon.

This division is loosely based on the Nintendo Cup '98, a real-world tournament held in Japan in July-August 1998. The rules in the real tournament, and in this game, are as follows:

  • The player will select three of their six Pokémon to battle with.
  • Pokémon must be between levels 1 and 30.
  • If one Pokémon on the opposing team is asleep, any moves that can put a second opposing Pokémon to sleep will fail to do so. The player can still inflict a second sleep status on their own party using Rest.
  • If one Pokémon on the opposing team is frozen, any moves that can freeze a second opposing Pokémon will fail to do so.
  • If the player and the opponent both have only one Pokémon left, and one Pokémon uses Selfdestruct or Explosion to knock out both Pokémon, the player who used Selfdestruct or Explosion will lose the match.
  • Two Pokémon of the same species cannot be entered.

It is normally impossible to get Venusaur, Charizard, Blastoise, Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, or Dragonite below level 30, meaning they are normally ineligible for this tournament. The long-range trainer glitch in Red, Green, Blue, and Yellow allows the player to get any Pokémon at very low levels. I am currently unsure if the seven aforementioned Pokémon can be entered if they are below level 30, but text in the game indicates only 33 of the 40 Pokémon in Stadium 0 can be entered, so I would assume they cannot be entered at all. Please contact me to provide an update if you know the answer.

Completing the Master Ball Cup will give the player access to the Doduo Game Boy if it is the first tournament to be completed, and the Dodrio Game Boy if it is the second. The Doduo Game Boy allows a Game Boy game played through Stadium 0 to run at twice the speed, and the Dodrio Game Boy allows it to run at three times the speed. Additionally, if the player clears the Master Ball Cup with a Pikachu in their party, Pikachu will be given the option to learn Surf.
