Crystal Colosseum

This colosseum becomes available to the player after they clear Waterfall Colosseum. In this colosseum, the player must defeat four Trainers, followed by the Colosseum Leader, in Tournament Battles. In Tournament Battles, the player starts the challenge as part of a sixteen-person tournament-style bracket. After winning a battle, the player advances to the next round in the bracket, and will face the Trainer who won their round in the bracket as well. The player must defeat all four opponents in the bracket to be able to face the Colosseum Leader. Which Trainers advance in the tournament bracket appears to be decided randomly, meaning the player can face any of the other fifteen Trainers in the bracket in the appropriate rounds.

The player can use a Rental Pass with rental Pokémon or a custom Battle Pass with Pokémon from a connected copy of the DS games Diamond, Pearl, Platinum, HeartGold, and SoulSilver.

The first time the player challenges this colosseum, they will have to face Double Battles, using the Level 30 Open ruleset, on Rank 2. After the colosseum has been completed for the first time, the player can fight through ranks 3-8 in Single or Double battles. Regardless of which battle type is chosen, winning on the selected rank will advance the rank by 1. After clearing all eight ranks, any subsequent challenges to this colosseum will always be Rank 8.

After the player completes Stargazer Colosseum the first time, the Level 50 All ruleset is unlocked for this colosseum. The player's starting rank on Level 50 All is set to 1, and is maintained separately from their rank in Level 30 Open. The rank can be advanced by Single or Double Battles.

In Single Battles, the player and opposing Trainers all have six Pokémon, and must choose three to participate in the battle with. In Doubles, all Trainers have six Pokémon, and must choose four.

The Pokémon that can be used by opposing Trainers differ depending on the level mode being played, and whether the player is playing Singles or Doubles. The links below will show the Pokémon pools used by each.

  • Level 30 Open, Singles
  • Level 30 Open, Doubles
  • Level 50 All, Singles
  • Level 50 All, Doubles

Completing this colosseum for the first time unlocks Sunny Park Colosseum.

Rank 2 Trainers

The Trainers and their Pokémon seen during the player's first challenge always have the same moves and item, but random IVs and Natures.
