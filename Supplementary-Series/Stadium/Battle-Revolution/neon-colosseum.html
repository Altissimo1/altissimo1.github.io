Neon Colosseum

This colosseum becomes available to the player after they clear Waterfall Colosseum. In this colosseum, the player must defeat six Trainers, followed by the Colosseum Leader, in Fortune Battles. In a Fortune Battle, all twelve of the Pokémon in both the player's and opposing Trainer's parties are added to a roulette wheel. The player and opposing Trainer will take turns dropping roulette balls into the spinning wheel to select Pokémon, starting with the player, until both sides have the number of Pokémon needed for the match. If either Trainer drops a ball on a Pokémon that has already been selected, a random Pokémon will be selected. Once all Pokémon have been selected, the Trainers will engage in a normal Knockout Battle.

The player can use a Rental Pass with rental Pokémon or a custom Battle Pass with Pokémon from a connected copy of the DS games Diamond, Pearl, Platinum, HeartGold, and SoulSilver.

The first time the player challenges this colosseum, they will have to face Double Battles, using the Level 30 Open ruleset, on Rank 2. After the colosseum has been completed for the first time, the player can fight through ranks 3-8 in Single or Double Battles. Regardless of which battle type is chosen, winning on the selected rank will advance the rank by 1. After clearing all eight ranks, any subsequent challenges to this colosseum will always be Rank 8.

After the player completes Stargazer Colosseum the first time, the Level 50 All ruleset is unlocked for this colosseum. The player's starting rank on Level 50 All is set to 1, and is maintained separately from their rank in Level 30 Open. The rank can be advanced by Single or Double Battles.

In Single Battles, the player and opposing Trainers all have six Pokémon, and must choose three from the roulette. In Doubles, all Trainers have six Pokémon, and must choose four.

The Pokémon that can be used by opposing Trainers differ depending on the level mode being played, and whether the player is playing Singles or Doubles. The links below will show the Pokémon pools used by each.
