Battle Facilities

The term "Battle Facilities" is a catch-all that refers to the Battle Tower, Battle Frontier, and other related facilities. Battle facilities typically follow these rules, with occasional exceptions:

  • The player enters three Pokémon (for Singles), three or four Pokémon (for Doubles), or two Pokémon (for Multis).
  • The player cannot enter two of the same species, or two Pokémon with the same held item. Opposing Trainers will not use two of the same species, or two Pokémon with the same held item.
  • Items cannot be used in battle.
  • Boosts such as the stat or type boosts provided by Badges and the benefits provided by Affection do not apply.
  • Opposing Trainers will often end up at a point where they have 31 IVs across all Pokémon, and have a full set of 510 EVs.
  • The player's win streak (number of Trainers defeated in a row) is usually tracked and kept somewhere where the player can see it.
  • Usually, the species of Pokémon that can be used by enemy Trainers are taken from the entire available Pokédex for the game, even including species that the player can only get in that game by trading.

The following games have a facility that follows under this definition:

  • Crystal Battle Tower
  • Ruby & Sapphire Battle Tower
  • Emerald Battle Frontier
  • Diamond & Pearl Battle Tower
  • Platinum Battle Frontier
  • HeartGold & SoulSilver Battle Frontier
  • Black & White Battle Subway
  • Black 2 & White 2 Battle Subway
  • X & Y Battle Maison
  • Omega Ruby & Alpha Sapphire Battle Maison
  • Sun & Moon Battle Tree
  • Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon Battle Tree

Crystal Battle Tower

The Battle Tower was originally added to Crystal as a feature that could be unlocked with access to the Mobile System GB, a Japan-only method of connecting to the Internet with a mobile phone. The discontinuation of the Mobile System GB means the Battle Tower is inaccessible in Japanese versions of the game (including the 2018 rerelease for the 3DS Virtual Console). In international releases of the game (including the 2018 rereleases), the Battle Tower is available to everyone.

In this version of the Battle Tower, there are ten possible challenges, starting with Level 10 and proceeding in increments of 10, up to 100. Each challenge has its own set of Pokémon that can be used by opposing Trainers. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7, and unlike future games, the game does not keep track of the player's total win streak past 7. All battles are Single Battles, as other types of battle were not present in Crystal.

The Pokémon that can be faced, and some more information about the tower's rules, can be found here.

Ruby and Sapphire Battle Tower

In this version of the Battle Tower, there are two modes: Level 50 and Level 100, which determine the level of opposing Pokémon. Each mode has its own set of Trainers and their Pokémon. Abilities for opposing Pokémon that have two are random. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7. All battles are Single Battles.

Additionally, all Pokémon that can be used by opposing Trainers are only those which can be found in the Hoenn Pokédex, which consists of 200 Pokémon.

Emerald Battle Frontier

In Emerald, a whole area called the Battle Frontier was introduced, which has seven facilities that use a variety of different gimmicks and rules. Trainers and Pokémon are shared across all facilities. Abilities for opposing Pokémon that have two are random. All facilities provide the option for level 50 or Open Level (where the opposing Pokémon match the highest level of the player's selected Pokémon). The available level modes, the number of battles that are included in a round, and the types of battles the player can play (Singles, Doubles, or Multi) depends on the specific facility.

The facilities in this version of the Battle Frontier are:

  • Battle Tower: The normal type of Battle Tower facility that was used in Crystal, Ruby, and Sapphire. Allows for Single, Double, and Multi Battles (either with a computer-controlled Trainer or another person with a copy of Emerald). Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Dome: The player can see the opponent's Pokémon before a match, and can only bring two Pokémon to fight two of the opponent's. Allows for Singles and Doubles. Battles are conducted in rounds of 4, using a tournament-style 16-person bracketing system.
  • Battle Palace: The player's Pokémon act on their own according to their Nature and moveset, and the only action the player can make is to switch them out or choose a replacement Pokémon after one faints. Allows for Singles and Doubles. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Arena: Pokémon cannot be switched out. If three turns pass and neither Pokémon has fainted, the two Pokémon will be judged on their performance using three categories, and the loser will be made to automatically faint. Only Singles are supported. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Factory: The player must choose three Pokémon from a set of six randomly-generated Pokémon (using the same list of Pokémon that can be fought as opponents) to battle with, and can swap for one of the opponent's after every battle. Allows for Singles and Doubles. Open Level sets all Pokémon to level 100. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Pike: The player must choose one of three rooms using a hint, where they may fight wild Pokémon, fight enemy Trainers (in Single or Double Battles), have their Pokémon healed, or have their Pokémon inflicted with status. Only one mode is available, where both Single and Double Battles may be faced depending on the room entered. There are 7 rooms that the player has to choose in the challenge.
  • Battle Pyramid: The player must navigate several floors in a dark maze to find the exit, and may face enemy Trainers (in Single or Double Battles) or wild Pokémon in the process. Only one mode is available, where both Single and Double Battles may be faced depending on the orientation of Trainers in the maze. The player must clear 7 floors per round.

Diamond and Pearl Battle Tower

In this version of the Battle Tower, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher than level 50 will have their level set back to 50 (retaining their EVs). The player can engage in Single Battles, Double Battles, or Multi Battles (either with a computer-controlled Trainer or another person with a copy of Diamond, Pearl, Platinum, HeartGold, or SoulSilver). Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.

Platinum, HeartGold, and SoulSilver Battle Frontier

In Platinum, the Battle Frontier makes its return from Emerald, with five facilities as opposed to Emerald's seven. HeartGold and SoulSilver use the exact same Frontier, with the only difference being the fixing of a bug that affects the Factory Frontier Brain's possible teams. Trainers are shared across all facilities. There are two sets of Pokémon: one used by the Tower, Arcade, Castle, and Factory, and a second used exclusively by the Hall. Abilities for opposing Pokémon that have two are random. All facilities provide the option for Single Battles, Double Battles, or Multi Battles (with another person with a copy of Platinum, HeartGold, or SoulSilver). The available level options and the number of battles that are included in a round depends on the specific facility.

The facilities in this version of the Battle Frontier are:

  • Battle Tower: The normal type of Battle Tower facility. Multi Battles conducted here can also occur with a computer-controlled trainer. All opposing Pokémon are level 50, and all of the player's Pokémon higher than 50 are set to 50. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Arcade: Before every battle, the player must spin a roulette to determine an effect that will apply to the upcoming battle or the roulette itself. All opposing Pokémon are level 50, and all of the player's Pokémon higher than 50 are set to 50; two roulette options may level the player or opponent's Pokémon up by 3. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Castle: After every battle, the player is awarded points, which can be used to heal Pokémon, scout the next Trainer's team, or give Pokémon held items, among other things. All opposing Pokémon are level 50, and all of the player's Pokémon higher than 50 are set to 50; the player may use their points to level the opponent's Pokémon up or down by 5. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.
  • Battle Hall: The player can only enter one Pokémon, and each opposing Trainer only has one Pokémon. In Doubles and Multis, the player enters two of the same species, and opposing Trainers use two of the same species. If a Pokémon can have two abilities, a Doubles or Multis opponent will have one Pokémon with each ability. Opposing Pokémon adjust their levels based on the player's Pokémon's level; the player cannot enter a Pokémon lower than level 30, and the opposing Pokémon will never exceed the player's Pokémon's level. Battles are conducted in rounds of 10.
  • Battle Factory: The player must choose three Pokémon from a set of six randomly-generated Pokémon (using the same list of Pokémon that can be fought as opponents in the Tower, Arcade, and Castle) to battle with, and can swap for one of the opponent's after every battle. There are two modes: Level 50 and Open Level (level 100). Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.

Black, White, Black 2, and White 2 Battle Subway

This facility functions as the Battle Tower did in previous games, with a different name. The facility is identical between Black & White and Black 2 & White 2. In this version of the concept, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher or lower than level 50 will have their level set to 50 (retaining their EVs). Abilities for opposing Pokémon that can have multiple are random, but do not include Hidden Abilities. The player can engage in Single Battles, Double Battles, or Multi Battles (either with a computer-controlled Trainer or another person with a copy of Black, White, Black 2, or White 2). Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.

This version of the facility additionally introduces two modes: the 'regular' version of a mode, and the 'Super' version. The 'regular' version only allows the player to play 21 battles (3 rounds) before ending, but the 'Super' version can be challenged indefinitely.

Information about possible opposing Pokémon can be found here, and information about opposing Trainers (including the IVs that each Trainer will have for their Pokémon) can be found here.

X, Y, Omega Ruby, and Alpha Sapphire Battle Maison

This facility functions as the Battle Subway did in Black, White, Black 2, and White 2, with a different name. The facility is identical between X & Y and Omega Ruby & Alpha Sapphire, although the computer Trainers that can be used in Multis are different. In this version of the concept, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher than level 50 will have their level set back to 50 (retaining their EVs). Abilities for opposing Pokémon that can have multiple are random, and can include Hidden Abilities. Opposing Trainers do not use Mega Evolution. The player can engage in Single Battles, Double Battles, Triple Battles (with 6 Pokémon), Rotation Battles (with 4 Pokémon), or Multi Battles (either with a computer-controlled Trainer or another person with a copy of X, Y, Omega Ruby, or Alpha Sapphire).

Unlike all previous facilities, there are no "rounds" in the Battle Maison. Instead, the player can simply stop after any battle of their choice and do anything else on the save file that they wish before resuming.

The 'Normal' version of a mode only allows the player to play 20 battles before ending, as opposed to the Battle Subway's 21.

Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun, and Ultra Moon Battle Tree

This facility functions as the Battle Maison did in X, Y, Omega Ruby, and Alpha Sapphire, with a different name. The facility is almost identical in all versions, but some Pokémon and Trainer rosters differ slightly between Sun & Moon and Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon, and some enemy Trainers may only appear in certain versions (either Sun & Ultra Sun versus Moon & Ultra Moon, or Sun & Moon versus Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon). In this version of the concept, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher than level 50 will have their level set back to 50 (retaining their EVs). In Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon only, the 'Normal' mode allows the player to enter Pokémon at any level, while the opponents stay at level 50. Abilities for opposing Pokémon that can have multiple are random, and can include Hidden Abilities. Opposing Pokémon sets that are holding a Z item or a Mega Stone can use a Z-Move or be Mega Evolved. The player can engage in Single Battles, Double Battles, or Multi Battles (either with a computer-controlled Trainer or another person with a copy of Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun, or Ultra Moon).

Sword and Shield Battle Tower

In this version of the concept, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher than level 50 will have their level set back to 50 (retaining their EVs). Abilities for opposing Pokémon that can have multiple are random, and can include Hidden Abilities. A small number of opposing Trainers are capable of Dynamaxing their Pokémon, and Leon can Gigantamax his Charizard. The player can engage in Single Battles or Double Battles.

Unlike previous facilities, this version of the concept introduces 'ranks' which the player must advance through. There are 10 ranks and a 'MAX' rank that can only be reached after completing the other 10. The player must complete a specific number of wins at each rank in order to advance to the next. Once a rank has been achieved, losing a battle will not demote the player to a previous rank. Once the player has achieved Max Rank, the battles here can be completed indefinitely and do not affect the player's rank.

Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl Battle Tower

In this version of the concept, there is only a level 50 mode, and all Pokémon higher than level 50 will have their level set back to 50 (retaining their EVs). Abilities for opposing Pokémon that can have multiple are fixed. The player can engage in Single Battles or Double Battles. Battles are conducted in rounds of 7.

This version of the Battle Tower has two modes: Regular Class and Master Class. The goal of the Regular Class is to win 49 matches in a row (7 rounds). After that point, the player cannot progress further in the Regular Class.

In the Master Class, the player increases their rank after completing each round of 7 battles, up to a maximum of Rank 10. Unlike in Sword and Shield, losing battles will result in the player's rank being decreased; the number of losses required to lower the player's rank depends on their current rank.

Supplementary Series Tournaments

Some of the supplementary games in the Pokémon series (the Stadium series, Colosseum, and XD) have their own tournaments that follow similar rules as the Battle Tower. The primary differences here are:

  1. The player and opponent both bring six Pokémon and must choose three or four to do battle with. The opponent will always have the same six Pokémon to choose from, which the player can view before selecting their team.
  2. There are a finite number of battles in each tournament with pre-defined Trainers.

However, the other standard rules of the Battle Tower tend to remain in place: the player cannot use items; all Pokémon are the same level; two Pokémon of the same species or with the same held item cannot be brought by either the player or their opponent. Stadium 0

In this version of the concept, only a total of 40 Pokémon are allowed to be used in either of the tournaments. Pokémon are not set to a specific level when entered into either tournament; the player must make sure the Pokémon is at the desired level before entering it. In both tournaments, the player brings six Pokémon and can pick three to battle the opposing Trainer with. Pokémon can either be selected from a list of rentals or transferred from a connected copy of (Japanese) Red, Green, Blue, or Yellow. All battles are Single Battles.

The specific tournaments are as follows:

  • Level 1-30 Division & rentals: Entered Pokémon must be between levels 1 and 30. Venusaur, Charizard, Blastoise, Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, and Dragonite cannot be used.
  • Level 50-55 Division & rentals: Entered Pokémon must be between levels 50 and 55, and the combined levels of the three chosen Pokémon cannot exceed 155.

Stadium 1

In this version of the concept, Pokémon are not set to a specific level when entered into any tournament; the player must make sure the Pokémon is at the desired level before entering it. In all tournaments, the player brings six Pokémon and can pick three to battle the opposing Trainer with. Pokémon can either be selected from a list of rentals or transferred from a connected copy of Red, Green, Blue, or Yellow. All battles are Single Battles.

The specific tournaments are as follows:

  • Pika Cup & rentals: Entered Pokémon must be between levels 15 and 20, and the combined levels of the three chosen Pokémon cannot exceed 50.
  • Petit Cup & rentals: Entered Pokémon must be small, un-evolved Pokémon between levels 25 and 30, and the combined levels of the three chosen Pokémon cannot exceed 80.
  • Poké Cup & rentals: This cup only exists in international releases. Entered Pokémon must be between level 50 and 55, and the combined levels of the three chosen Pokémon cannot exceed 155.
  • Prime Cup & rentals: Opposing Pokémon are all level 100.
  • Nintendo Cup '97 & rentals: This cup only exists in Japanese releases. Entered Pokémon must be between level 50 and 55, and the combined levels of the three chosen Pokémon cannot exceed 155.
  • Nintendo Cup '98 & rentals: This cup only exists in Japanese releases. Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 30, and only certain Pokémon are eligible.
  • Nintendo Cup '99 & rentals: This cup only exists in Japanese releases. Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50, and there is a small ban list.
  • Gym Leader Castle & rentals: The opponents' Pokémon will all match the level of the player's highest-level Pokémon. If the player's highest-level Pokémon is lower than 50, all opposing Pokémon will be level 50.

  • Gym leader Castle & rentals: The opponents' Pokémon will all match the level of the player's highest-level Pokémon. If the player's highest-level Pokémon is lower than 50, all opposing Pokémon will be level 50.

Colosseum

The tournaments that use Tower-like rules in Colosseum are all part of Battle Mode. Pokémon are not set to a specific level when entered into any tournament; the player must the player must make sure the Pokémon is at the desired level before entering it. Abilities of opposing Pokémon are fixed. In all Singles tournaments, the player brings six Pokémon and can pick three to battle the opposing Trainer with; in all Doubles tournaments, both Trainers pick four. Pokémon can either be selected from the Pokémon the player has obtained in Story Mode or transferred from a connected copy of Ruby, Sapphire, Emerald, FireRed, or LeafGreen. The type of battle depends on the tournament selected.

  • Mt. Battle (Singles): The opponents' Pokémon will all match the level of the player's highest-level Pokémon. If the player's highest-level Pokémon is lower than 50, all opposing Pokémon will be level 50.
  • Mt. Battle (Doubles): The opponents' Pokémon will all match the level of the player's highest-level Pokémon. If the player's highest-level Pokémon is lower than 50, all opposing Pokémon will be level 50.
  • Phenac Stadium (Singles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Phenac Stadium (Doubles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Pyrite Colosseum (Singles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Pyrite Colosseum (Doubles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Under Colosseum (Singles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Under Colosseum (Doubles): Entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50.
  • Under Colosseum (Singles): There are two modes: level 50 and level 100. In the level 50 mode, entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50; in the level 100 mode; Pokémon of any level can be entered.
  • Under Colosseum (Doubles): There are two modes: level 50 and level 100. In the level 50 mode, entered Pokémon must be between level 1 and 50; in the level 100 mode; Pokémon of any level can be entered.
  • Tower Colosseum (Singles): Opposing Pokémon are all level 100.
  • Tower Colosseum (Doubles): Opposing Pokémon are all level 100.

Battle Revolution

In this version of the concept, there are two level modes the player can participate in for most of the colosseums: Level 30 Open, where opposing Pokémon match the player's highest-leveled Pokémon but do not go lower than 30, and Level 50 All, where all of the player's and opponent's Pokémon are set to level 50. One colosseum, Sunny Park Colosseum, also offers the Level 5 Max ruleset, where Pokémon must be between levels 1 and 5. In all colosseums, the player brings six Pokémon, but the number of Pokémon the player battles with depends on the colosseum and whether the player is engaged in Singles or Doubles. In most colosseums, Pokémon can either be selected from a pre-set Rental Pass or transferred from a connected copy of Diamond, Pearl, Platinum, HeartGold, or SoulSilver.

The first attempt at any of the colosseums' challenges will always result in the same enemy Trainers and Pokémon. Stargazer Colosseum has pre-set teams for all of its Trainers. Apart from this, Trainers are given Pokémon from a randomly selected pool, making the Battle Revolution challenges more similar to the Battle Tower than the other challenges in the supplementary games.

The specific colosseums are as follows:

  • Gateway Colosseum: The player can only use Rental Passes. After completing Stargazer Colosseum, the player gains the ability to trade Pokémon with the opposing Trainer after a battle, and the traded Pokémon is kept permanently on the Rental Pass. Allows for only Level 30 Open, but all rental Pokémon are level 50.
  • Main Street Colosseum: Allows for Level 30 Open and Level 50 All.
  • Waterfall Colosseum: The player uses five Pokémon in Singles or six in Doubles and defeats the opposing Trainer in a best-of series of one-on-one or two-on-two matches. Allows for Level 30 Open and Level 50 All.
  • Crystal Colosseum: Allows for Level 30 Open and Level 50 All.
  • Neon Colosseum: The player's and opponent's Pokémon are all placed on a roulette wheel, and both sides pick Pokémon from the roulette before the battle. Allows for Level 30 Open and Level 50 All.
  • Sunny Park Colosseum: Before completing Stargazer Colosseum, allows for Level 30 Open. After completing it, allows for only Level 5 Max. Rental Passes cannot be used in Level 5 Max.
  • Magma Colosseum: Trainers receive points for how many Pokémon are not fainted at the end of a battle, and the Trainer with the highest score after 5 rounds fights the Colosseum Leader. Allows for Level 30 Open and Level 50 All.
  • Sunset Colosseum: A random pool of twelve Pokémon is generated and both Trainers choose their teams from this pool. Allows for Level 30 Open (where Pokémon are level 30) or Level 50 All.
  • Courtyard Colosseum: Before completing Stargazer Colosseum, allows for Level 30 Open. After completing it, the challenge becomes a 100-Trainer survival gauntlet where the player spins a wheel to restore their Pokémon's HP, PP, status, or held item after each battle. This challenge allows for Level 30 Open or Level 50 All.
  • Stargazer Colosseum: The first time it is faced, allows for Level 30 Open. After completing it, allows only for Level 50 All.

Information about the possible opposing Pokémon is shown below:

  • Rentals: The default Rental Passes.
  • Pokémon (Lv.30 Open, Singles): Pokémon used by opposing Trainers in Main Street, Waterfall, Neon, Crystal, Sunny Park, Magma, Sunset, and Courtyard Colosseums for battles in Lv.30 Open Singles.
  • Pokémon (Lv.30 Open, Doubles): Pokémon used by opposing Trainers in Main Street, Waterfall, Neon, Crystal, Sunny Park, Magma, Sunset, and Courtyard Colosseums for battles in Lv.30 Open Doubles.
  • Pokémon (Lv.50 Open, Singles): Pokémon used by opposing Trainers in Main Street, Waterfall, Neon, Crystal, Magma, and Sunset Colosseums for battles in Lv.50 Open Singles.
  • Pokémon (Lv.50 Open, Doubles): Pokémon used by opposing Trainers in Main Street, Waterfall, Neon, Crystal, Magma, and Sunset Colosseums for battles in Lv.50 Open Doubles.
  • Gateway Colosseum Pokémon (Singles)
  • Gateway Colosseum Pokémon (Doubles)
  • Sunny Park Colosseum Pokémon (Singles)
  • Sunny Park Colosseum Pokémon (Doubles)
  • Courtyard Colosseum Pokémon (Lv.30 Open, Singles)
  • Courtyard Colosseum Pokémon (Lv.30 Open, Doubles)
  • Courtyard Colosseum Pokémon (Lv.50 All, Singles)
  • Courtyard Colosseum Pokémon (Lv.50 All, Doubles)

Emerald

Slateport City, Verdanturf Town, and Fallarbor Town contain Battle Tents: facilities which allow the player to play using the mechanics of one of the Battle Frontier's facilities. Unlike the Battle Frontier, Pokémon here are all taken from the Hoenn Pokédex, and the game does not track a streak for winning multiple rounds in a row.

  • Slateport City (Battle Factory)
  • Verdanturf Town (Battle Palace)
  • Fallarbor Town (Battle Arena)

Emerald also features Trainer Hill, a series of Double Battles where the player is encouraged to complete the required battles quickly. In the Japanese releases, the Trainers in this facility are dictated by scanning e-Reader cards; internationally, the Trainers faced are always the same. Enemy Trainers' Pokémon will always match the level of the player's highest-level Pokémon. FireRed & LeafGreen

Colosseum

XD: Gale of Darkness

However, the goal of this facility is only to defeat five Trainers in a row, but to obtain a 'high score' by playing well against the opponent. Information about the scoring can be found here.

In Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire only, the player can take the Mauville Food Court challenge in Mauville City. This challenge expects the player to defeat enemy Trainers in a specific number of turns. Information about this challenge can be found here.

Sword & Shield

The goal of this challenge is to defeat five Trainers using a team of Pokémon that all must share at least one common type, with only two full heals allotted to the player. Although the player only needs to defeat five Trainers, the game will track the player's highest recorded streak for any one type. Opposing Pokémon for Restricted Sparring can be found here.

Let's Go Pikachu & Let's Go Eevee

Let's Go Pikachu & Let's Go Eevee feature Master Trainers, one for every species of Pokémon the player can obtain in the game, that each only have one Pokémon. The player must defeat each Trainer using only the same species of Pokémon as that Trainer has, in a similar design to that used by the Battle Hall in Platinum's Battle Frontier. Information about opposing Trainers can be found here.

Legends: Arceus

The free Daybreak update for Legends: Arceus introduced the Path of Solitude. Here, a single Pokémon must defeat a single enemy Pokémon to clear the challenge, in a similar design to that used by the Battle Hall in Platinum's Battle Frontier. Information about opposing Pokémon can be found here.
