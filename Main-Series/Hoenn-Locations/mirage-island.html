Mirage Island

Access to:
Technically part of Route 130

Utility:
Surf is required to access Mirage Island.

Access to:
All Mirage Islands can only be accessed from soaring in the sky. Their location on the map is given below, in relation to each individual island.

Utility:
The Mach Bike is required to access a Max Elixir in Spot #4.
Rock Smash can be used to find items and encounter wild Pokémon in Spots #6 and #8.
Cut is required to access a Rare Candy in Spot #7.

A man in the southeastern house in Pacifidlog Town can be spoken to to reveal if Mirage Island is visible.

Every Pokémon has a personality value which controls some aspects of the Pokémon, such as its gender, Ability, shiny status, and in the case of Spinda, its spot pattern. Every day, the game will generate a value between 0 and 65535 and compare this to the personality values of the Pokémon in the player's party. If a match is found - which on a single Pokémon is a 1/65536 chance - Mirage Island will be visible and accessible. The more Pokémon the player has, the more likely it is that a match will be found, but Mirage Island will only be visible if a matching Pokémon is actively in the player's party.

Every day, the game picks one random Mirage Spot (Mirage Island, Mirage Forest, Mirage Cave, Mirage Mountain, or Crescent Isle) to be accessible. More can be made available on a given day by interacting with other players through StreetPass. There are eight of each of Mirage Island, Mirage Forest, Mirage Cave, and Mirage Mountain, located in different areas; despite having the same name, their Pokémon and items differ, meaning the player must visit all eight to get everything. Details of all eight Mirage Islands are given below.
