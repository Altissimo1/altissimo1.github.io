Battle Maison Trainers

In the Battle Maison, enemy Trainers - their Trainer class and name - are pulled from a pre-existing list of 300 Trainers. Every Trainer in this list has a number, an IV value, and a Pokémon roster associated with them. The number determines which battle numbers the player may face this Trainer as an opponent in; lower numbers will appear in earlier battles than higher numbers. The IV value will determine what IVs the Pokémon belonging to this Trainer can possess. The enemy Trainer's three Pokémon will be a random selection of Pokémon from their roster, although they will never use two Pokémon of the same species or two Pokémon with the same held item.

The Trainers that the player can team up with for Multi Battles also have rosters associated with them, shown below. Like enemy Trainers, Multis Trainers can never have two Pokémon of the same species, or with the same held item. To the best of my knowledge, the Multis Trainers will always have 31 IVs.

This page does not include data on the Battle Chatelaines; that information is covered on the Battle Maison page.

Battle Maison Group Mechanics

Trainer groups each have a specific IV value associated with them. Pokémon that belong to a Trainer will receive the IVs of the Trainer's group. The IV values of the groups are given below:

  • 1-50: 3IV
  • 51-70: 7IV
  • 71-90: 11IV
  • 91-110: 15IV
  • 111-160: 19IV
  • 161-180: 23IV
  • 181-200: 27IV
  • 201-300: 31IV

As outlined on the Battle Maison Pokémon page, Pokémon are grouped into tiers. There are four tiers: Low, Mid, High, and Legendary. For each Pokémon in a tier, it will additionally have multiple movesets. Pokémon in the Low and Mid Tier have two, and those in the High and Legendary Tiers have four.

The rosters of Trainers 1-220 are set up such that each Trainer uses only Pokémon belonging to a specific tier, and then a specific set within that tier. Trainers 221-300 have some Trainers that can use Pokémon from multiple sets, as well as Legendaries. The Trainers are set up such that Trainers in earlier battles use Pokémon from weaker tiers than those in later battles. The exact progression of tiers and groups of Trainers is as follows:

Multis Trainers

When taking on the Multis challenge, the player has the ability to play with a friend with a second 3DS and a copy of X, Y, Omega Ruby, or Alpha Sapphire, or with a computer-controlled trainer whose Pokémon are predetermined. Computer-controlled characters will never have two Pokémon of the same species or with the same held item, and their Pokémon always have 31 IVs.

X/Y

By default, the player has access to three Trainers: Shauna, Tierno, and Trevor. Additional Trainers can be added if the player registers Friend Codes on their system. Each Trainer added this way will have two Pokémon, randomly selected from the list of Battle Maison Pokémon. Only Pokémon from the High and Legendary Tiers will be selected. The selected set is usually Set-3, but if this would result in both of the Trainer's Pokémon sharing the same held item, one of the sets will be changed to another set for that Pokémon.

Pokémon will always match the gender of their Trainer, unless they are genderless or can only be one gender.

The Pokémon used by Shauna, Tierno, and Trevor seem to always use their first Ability. The same may be true of Pokémon used by Friend Code Trainers, but I have been unable to confirm this.
