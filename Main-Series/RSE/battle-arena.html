Battle Arena

The Battle Arena is one of seven facilities located in the Battle Frontier, accessible only in Emerald. The Frontier Brain here is Greta.

Modes

The Battle Arena only allows for Single Battles. The player can enter three Pokémon.

Mechanics

Gimmick

Neither the player nor the opponent is allowed to switch Pokémon, and Pokémon will always be sent out in the order they are listed in the party. The two Pokémon on the field will engage in three turns' worth of combat. At the end of three turns, if neither Pokémon has fainted, the game will judge which Pokémon performed better using criteria listed below. The winning Pokémon stays on the field for the next round, and the losing Pokémon faints.

If either Pokémon faints before three turns are up, the next Pokémon will be sent out, and the next round begins.

General

Mechanics that apply to every Facility are on the Battle Frontier page. Here are mechanics specific to the Battle Arena:

  • Two Pokémon of the same species, or with the same held item, cannot be entered. Opposing Trainers will never use two Pokémon of the same species, or with the same held item.
  • Items cannot be used during battle.
  • The player's Pokémon will be automatically healed after every battle.
  • Battles are conducted in rounds of 7. The player can save between battles in a round, but will not be able to do anything except complete the rest of the round when they load the game. The player can do anything between rounds without it affecting their streak.
  • Items used or swapped (via Trick, Thief, Covet, or Knock Off) during the battle will be given back to the Pokémon after the battle.

Scoring Mechanics

Judging a Pokémon's performance is based on three categories: Mind, Skill, and Body.

  • Mind: Almost any move that deals damage is awarded 1 point. The exceptions are Counter, Bide, and Mirror Coat, which award 0 points, and Fake Out, which awards -1 point. Protect, Detect, and Endure award -1 point. All other moves award 0 points. The Pokémon with the higher point total wins the judgment for Mind.
  • Skill: If a move fails to affect the opponent because of the opponent's ability, the user is awarded -3 points. If a move fails to affect the opponent for any other reason (type-based immunity, missing, etc), the user is awarded -2 points. The exception is if the move fails due to the opponent using Protect or Detect, in which case 0 points are awarded. If a move hits and is super-effective, the user is awarded 2 points. If the move hits and is not very effective, the user is awarded -1 point. In all other cases, the user is awarded 1 point. The Pokémon with the higher point total wins the judgment for Skill.
  • Body: The game completes the following calculation for each Pokémon: (Pokémon's current HP * 100) / Pokémon's HP when the round started. Whichever Pokémon has the highest value wins the judgment for Body.
