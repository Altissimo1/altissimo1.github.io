Battle Subway Trainers

In the Battle Subway, enemy Trainers - their Trainer class and name - are pulled from a pre-existing list of 300 Trainers. Every Trainer in this list has a number, an IV value, and a Pokémon roster associated with them. The number determines which train and which sets of 7 battles the player may face this Trainer as an opponent in; lower numbers will appear in earlier battles than higher numbers. The IV value will determine what IVs the Pokémon belonging to this Trainer can possess. The enemy Trainer's three Pokémon will be a random selection of Pokémon from their roster, although they will never use two Pokémon of the same species or two Pokémon with the same held item.

The Trainers that the player can team up with for Multi Battles also have rosters associated with them, shown below. Like enemy Trainers, Multis Trainers can never have two Pokémon of the same species, or with the same held item. To the best of my knowledge, the Multis Trainers will always have 31 IVs.

This page does not include data on the Subway Bosses.

Trainers 1-110 are only in the Normal Train, and Trainers 111-300 are only in the Super Train.

The numbers after the Pokémon's names refer to set numbers, explained in more detail on the Battle Subway Pokémon page.

Battle Subway Group Mechanics

Trainer groups each have a specific IV value associated with them. Pokémon that belong to a Trainer will receive the IVs of the Trainer's group. The IV values of the groups are given below:

  • 1-50: 3IV
  • 51-70: 7IV
  • 71-110: 11IV
  • 111-160: 19IV
  • 161-180: 23IV
  • 181-200: 27IV
  • 201-220: 31IV
  • 221-300: 31IV

As outlined on the Battle Subway Pokémon page, Pokémon are grouped into tiers. There are four tiers: Low, Mid, High, and Legendary. For each Pokémon in a tier, it may additionally have multiple movesets. Pokémon in the Low and Mid Tiers have two, and those in the High and Legendary Tiers have four. Some Pokémon are in both the Mid and High tiers, giving them a total of six sets.

The rosters of Trainers 1-220 are set up such that each Trainer uses only Pokémon belonging to a specific tier, and then a specific set within that tier. Trainers 221-300 may use Pokémon from any High or Legendary set, and a few use Set-2 Pokémon from the Mid Tier. The Trainers are set up such that Trainers in earlier battles use Pokémon from weaker tiers than those in later battles. The exact progression of tiers and groups of Trainers is as follows:
