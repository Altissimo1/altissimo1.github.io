How Many Pokémon Can You Get with Only Pokémon Silver?

Assuming you do not use any glitches, the answer is 199. The Professor Oak challenge guide for Silver can be found here, although I've made a shorter list of encounters below in case anything happens to the external guide. I've also made one minor fact correction in my notes below: Wild Pokémon cannot be encountered using Rock Smash in Burned Tower, so you will have to pick up Shuckle with Rock Smash on Route 40 instead.

The list of Pokémon you cannot get without trading, which equals 52 species, is as follows:

Two Starters (6) - 6 total
As you can only select one starter Pokémon, the other two starters, and their evolutions, are unavailable; this totals 6 Pokémon.
Kanto Starters (9) - 15 total
None of the Kanto starters can be obtained without trading with Gen 1 games or using Pokémon Stadium.
Kanto Legendaries (4) - 19 total
Articuno, Zapdos, Moltres, and Mewtwo are unobtainable without trading with Gen 1 games.
Kanto Fossils (4) - 23 total
Omanyte, Omastar, Kabuto, and Kabutops are unobtainable without trading with Gen 1 games.
Trade Evolutions (10) - 33 total
Golem, Machamp, Alakazam, Gengar, Politoed, Slowking, Steelix, Scizor, Kingdra, and Porygon2 evolve by trade. Without the ability to trade, they are unobtainable.
Version Exclusives (10) - 43 total
The following Pokémon, and their evolutions, cannot be obtained without the use of glitches or trading: Spinarak, Growlithe, Mankey, Gligar, Teddiursa, and Mantine.
Stone Evolutions (7) - 50 total
You can only obtain one each of Fire Stone, Thunderstone, Water Stone, and Leaf Stone. There are 3 Pokémon that evolve with Fire Stone, 2 that evolve with Thunderstone, 4 that evolve with Water Stone, and 3 that evolve with Leaf Stone, for a total of 12. Growlithe is exclusive to Gold, which puts the total number in Silver at 11. Subtracting the four you are able to evolve, you are left with 7 inaccessible stone evolutions.
Mythical/Event (2) - 52 total
Outside of glitches, Mew and Celebi were only obtainable via events that are no longer active. However, they were never needed to get the certificate from the Game Freak employee.
